Elliptic Fourier Descriptor

1 Description of a Closed Contour

Any continuous closed curve CCC in the 2D plane can be expressed as a function f:R→R2f:\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^2f:R→R2, with

f(t)=(x(t)y(t))f(t)=\begin{pmatrix} x(t) \\ y(t) \end{pmatrix}f(t)=(x(t)y(t)​)

with the continuous parameter ttt being varied over the range [0,T][0,T][0,T] and f(0)=f(T)f(0)=f(T)f(0)=f(T). Note that x(t)x(t)x(t) and y(t)y(t)y(t) are independent, real-valued functions, and ttt is typically the path length along the curve.

Sampling a closed curve CCC at KKK randomly spaced positions t0,t1,...,tp,...tK−1t_0, t_1, ..., t_p, ... t_{K-1}t0​,t1​,...,tp​,...tK−1​, with Δtp=tp−tp−1\Delta t_p = t_p-t_{p-1}Δtp​=tp​−tp−1​, resulting a closed contour with a sequence of discrete 2D coordinates V=(v0,v1,...,vp,...,vK−1)V=(\boldsymbol{v}_0, \boldsymbol{v}_1, ..., \boldsymbol{v}_p, ..., \boldsymbol{v}_{K-1})V=(v0​,v1​,...,vp​,...,vK−1​), with

vp=(x(tp)y(tp))\boldsymbol{v}_p=\begin{pmatrix} x(t_p) \\ y(t_p) \end{pmatrix}vp​=(x(tp​)y(tp​)​)

Since the contour is closed, thus

vp=vp+kT\boldsymbol{v}_p=\boldsymbol{v}_{p+kT}vp​=vp+kT​

for 0≤p<K0 \le p < K0≤p<K and any k∈Zk \in \mathbb{Z}k∈Z.

Furthermore, a closed contour is typically regarded as piecewise linear when processing it using computer algorithm, which mean the curve between vp\boldsymbol{v}_pvp​ and vp−1\boldsymbol{v}_{p-1}vp−1​ is regarded as a straight line.

5.2 Invariance of Trannslation

A0A_0A0​ and C0C_0C0​ is simply the average of x and y coordinate when the contour is regularly sampled. To make Fourier coefficients invariant against translation, it is thus sufficient to zero A0A_0A0​ and C0C_0C0​, thereby shifting the shape's center to the origin of the coordinate system. Alternatively, it is achieved by simply ignoring coefficient A0A_0A0​ and C0C_0C0​.
